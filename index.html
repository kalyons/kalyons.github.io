My name is Kevin Lyons, a member of the Class of 2016 at Weymouth High School just south of Boston, Massachusetts. I have created this page to build my portfolio and showcase all of my interests. I consider myself extremely well ballanced between the spheres of academics, athletics, social scenes, commmunity outreach, professional work and independent research. I love to share my knowledge with others to engage personal thought and reflection, those "aha" moments we all crave.

I have a focus in computer science on the side of software development in applied sciences. Biochemical engineering and computational chemistry fields appeal to me as the ideal blend of CS with real-world, immediate application. Mathematical modeling and efficient computation are key concepts I consider when working in these disciplines.

Experiences

[Captain] Varsity Football

Soccer would be my go-to athletic event for much of my childhood, until my proliferation as a football player in the summer leading up to eighth grade. Since then, I have had so many gratifying experiences through the game and now start both sides of the ball as a senior captain. The physical aspect of this competition is certainly worth noting, yet so too are the mental and emotional. The lasting friendships I have formed as a result of my play shall stay with me long after my high school years.

[Captain] Varsity Basketball

The game of basketball has been integral to my development since as early as six years old. I am fortunate enough to come up through local town programs and the Weymouth High School Basketball Program to become a starting captain heading into my senior year. Furthermore, I have been able to coach the youth in my community in grades 2-4 for multiple years, thus giving back to a cause that meant so much to me.

[Captain] Math Team

My passion for math is evident dating back to primary school years, yet I never had an opportunity to truly allow it to shine until my junior year where I joined the Weymouth High School Math Team, dubbed weyMath. My educational experience since my decision to join has been completely changed for the better. With a refreshing group of intellectuals surrounding me, I was exposed to a different side of the social spectrum (in contrast with the largely athletic paradigm I lived in prior). I now see the best of both worlds - mental and physical strength - and am better suited to deal with both. Heading into my senior year, I am now a captain of this team. Coming off a divisional championship in 2015, our team is looking to stay sharp and maintain our high ranking. Thus, I am coordinating many practice opportunities in the offeason before we return to weekly drill routines in mid-September. Personal pride in the team's success is certainly my first priority, yet so too is individual achievement. I was awarded the 2015 Gold Medal for highest scorer on the team, including one perfect score to seal a victory for my team. I am also looking to coordinate additional competition opportunities for my team as is deserved. Administation and I are working during the summer of 2015 to compete at the Worcester Polytechnic Invitational Math Meet and the Harvard-MIT Mathematics Tournament in the upcoming fall season.
