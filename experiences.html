MIT Leadership Training Institute

  • As a result, went on to create the Beyond the Code program, which is detailed below

  • During the Closing Ceremonies of the program, I was selected as one of only three out of 50 students to present my findings in one of the largest lecture halls on campus.

  • Also had to compile elaborate documentation for this project

Beyond the Code

  • Founded the organization Beyond the Code in the spring of 2015, my junior year, as my assignment from the above mentioned MIT Leadership Training Institute

  • Beyond the Code is a unique computer science mentorship program that pairs knowledgeable and passionate high school students with those in grades 3-8 to develop an understanding of problem solving and logical thinking skills for future years.

  • Have reached 70 students to date, across 3 Weymouth Public Schools in grades 3-8

  • Recruited 10+ high-school student mentors working under my guidance to support the vision of my program

  • Worked alongside numerous district faculty, including the Director of Instructional Technology, Community Relations Liasion and principals at each of the three schools

  • Presented my program to the Weymouth School Committee to raise STEM Awareness

  • Plan in place for upcoming years to create a sustainable, replicable mentorship program across the town
Advocate for STEM Education and Exposure

STEM is an acronym for Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics.

As a result of my work with Beyond the Code, I have been afforded several unique opportunities to share my findings and continue the STEM discussion in professional forums:

  • Along with two female STEM peers, facilitated an hour-long CS workshop to a dozen female students in grades 5-8

  • I spoke in front of the Weymouth School Committee to discuss the impact of Beyond the Code within the larger district-wide effort to enhance STEM education for students at all ages. This public appearance allowed STEM education to be discussed in a new light for the community.

  • Delected by the Director of Instructional Technology of the Weymouth Public Schools as a student advisor for the local STEM Community Compact, where I emphasized student self-advocacy as a catalyst for creating new opportunities
Software Engineer Intern, LifeYield, LLC.

  • In the summer before my senior year, I completed an intensive paid internship at Boston-based financial computing firm LifeYield.

  • Given task in a four-week timespan to complete a platform release of a back-end application for request handling on a SOAP XML facade service

  • Allowed me to apply my knowledge of recursive definitions and file parsing while also picking up many new skills, including cross-thread UI callbacks, asynchronous iteration and improved model-view-controller separation

  • Took direction along the way from supervising developers and adapted as necessary to dynamic feedback

  • Presented final working application to supervisors in the Development and QA departments, as well as LifeYield's CEO and CTO

  • I compiled release notes for future reference, as well as an OpenSource release of select code definitons.

I will be returning to LifeYield for more work in the summer of 2016.
