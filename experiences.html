[Captain] Varsity Football

Soccer would be my go-to athletic event for much of my childhood, until my proliferation as a football player in the summer leading up to eighth grade. Since then, I have had so many gratifying experiences through the game and now start both sides of the ball as a senior captain. The physical aspect of this competition is certainly worth noting, yet so too are the mental and emotional. The lasting friendships I have formed as a result of my play shall stay with me long after my high school years.

[Captain] Math Team

My passion for math is evident dating back to primary school years, yet I never had an opportunity to truly allow it to shine until my junior year where I joined the Weymouth High School Math Team, dubbed weyMath. My educational experience since my decision to join has been completely changed for the better. With a refreshing group of intellectuals surrounding me, I was exposed to a different side of the social spectrum (in contrast with the largely athletic paradigm I lived in prior). I now see the best of both worlds - mental and physical strength - and am better suited to deal with both. Heading into my senior year, I am now a captain of this team. Coming off a divisional championship in 2015, our team is looking to stay sharp and maintain our high ranking. Thus, I am coordinating many practice opportunities on social media in the offeason before we return to weekly drill routines in mid-September. Personal pride in the team's success is certainly my first priority, yet so too is individual achievement. I was awarded the 2015 Gold Medal for highest scorer on the team, including one perfect score to seal a victory for my team. I am also looking to coordinate additional competition opportunities for my team as is deserved. Administation and I are working during the summer of 2015 to compete at the Worcester Polytechnic Institute Math Meet and the Harvard-MIT Mathematics Tournament in the upcoming fall season. I work with press relations and was responsible for announcing our team's success in local media outlets.

Software Engineer Intern, LifeYield, LLC.

I completed an intensive paid internship at Boston-based financial computing firm LifeYield, working toward a platform release of a back-end application for request handling on a SOAP XML facade service. This application is critical for the Development and Quality Assurance Departments in maintaining continuity across multiple versions and releases under a common gateway service. This work allowed me to apply my knowledge of recursive definitions file parsing while also picking up many new skills, including cross-thread UI callbacks, asynchronous iteration and improved model-view-controller separation. I was fortunate enough to present to development supervisors, including LifeYield's CEO and the CTO. I compiled release notes for future reference, as well as an OpenSource release of select code definitons.

I will be returning to LifeYield for more work in the summer of 2016.

MIT Leadership Training Institute

By far the most transformative experience of my career, the MIT Leadership Training Institute made me realize the deep potential I hold evoke change in those around me. An intensive yet cheerful 12-week program on MIT's Cambridge Campus, LTI's selective application process selected 50 deserving students from the metro-Boston region to participate. In what was hailed as "the most competitive applicant pool to date" by the program's managing director, so many amazing students flocked to campus every Sunday to discover their ability to make an impact within respective communities. After being instructed to find a need or shortcoming in my community and develop a solution, I founded the non-profit organization Beyond the Code. Please see the Beyond the Code documentation on my profile for more details. During the Closing Ceremonies of the program, I was selected as one of only three students to present my findings in one of the largest lecture halls on campus. I also had to compile elaborate documentation for this project. Ultimately, I met so many intelligent and motivated individuals who allowed me to fully come to terms with my potential, providing an impetous for outreach. Learn more...

Beyond the Code

I founded the non-profit organization Beyond the Code in the spring of 2015, my junior year. This is a unique computer science mentorship program that pairs knowledgeable and passionate high school students with those in younger grades, currently 3-8, to develop an understanding of problem solving and logical thinking skills for future years. The ability to code and "think like a programmer," as our slogan reads, is a universal skill I observed as non-existent. Within our first session in the Spring of 2015, we received a tremendous interest from youth, with registration filling up to capacity within 36 hours of initial release and ultimately reaching 70 students in our first three schools of operation. Parents, students and faculty alike praised the opportunity as well needed in the current technical age. I have plans in place to pass the role of director to an already-selected rising junior during SY 15-16, followed by an already-selected student for SY 16-17. This will ensure a sustainable, replicable mentorship program that shall continue to make a difference years after my graduation. Check out our website, Spring 2015 film and OpenSource curriculum.

[Director] Student Voice

Student Voice is a critical group on the Weymouth High School campus that seeks to empower students on a number of issues and encourage practical problem solving to meet administrators with resolution. Student Voice is responsible for holding the multi-annual Student Voice Summit, which will pick back up in the fall of SY 15-16. At the Summit, students from all over the school, spanning interests, disciplines and notorieties are selected by teachers and administrators for a day-long dynamic forum on current shortcomings in Weymouth High School culture and climate. Rather than becoming a session to vent about injustices done unto students over the years, the Summit compels student to reach meaningful solutions to issues including technology misuse, faculty-student relationships and media outlets for information. This past year myself and three other peers were named the new directors of the program by our principal and given the task to reinvigorate its decline over the previous school year. I look forward to collaborating across the school in such an open, inviting forum for positive long-term solutions that are genuinely student-born. We have a social media presence and a developing website.

STEM Advocacy to Higher Education Groups

As a result of my work with Beyond the Code, I have been afforded several unique opportunities to share my findings and continue the STEM discussion in professional forums:

(1) On Saturday, April 11th, 2015, myself and two female STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics) peers of mine presented an hour-long workshop to a dozen female students in grades 5-8. Our workshop on the basics of computer science aligned directly with the scheduled Beyond the Code curriculum and served a platform to test our activities on a smaller scale before we moved to a multi-school platform. Learn more...

(2) I spoke in front of the Weymouth School Committee on April 30th, 2015 to discuss the impact of Beyond the Code within the larger district-wide effort to enhance STEM education for students at all ages. This public appearance allowed STEM education to be discussed in a new light for the community.

(3) I am a student advisor for the local STEM Community Compact, which seeks to connect all aspects of the STEM spectrum, including but not limited to educators, families, students, local businesses, career specialists and college admissions officers to establish some standard of STEM readiness in public education. Special Olympics Volunteerism

The Weymouth chapter of the Massachusetts Special Olympics holds their annual games for the students in respective educational programs across the district, spanning ages 6 to 18. This annual event, held in May, is a wonderful chance for community members to celebrate the persistence and optimism of those around us who face so much. I have served a leadership role in three years of service at this event, coordinating closely with school administration to include fellow students to act as positive role models. Numerous physical activities ensue throughout the day, with my favorite moment being the participants in the Games walking through a wave of supporters in the Opening Ceremonies.

Rotary International Involvement

Local Rotary organizations highlight the best of citizenship and community outreach across all social scenes. Their motto of "Service Above Self" certainly resounds with me each and every day. I have benefitted from involvement in multiple Rotary-organized events:

(1) For the weekend of May 15-17, 2015, I attended the annual Rotary Youth Leadership Awards (RYLA) at the South Shore Sea Camps in Brewster, Massachusetts. With another group of compassionate individuals, we were able to connect our leadership experiences to that point while also learning invaluable lessons about altruism, understanding and being heard. The new connections that arose from this event were immediate as I now possess more outlets for advice that I know what to do with - a truly satisfying experience.

Sharing our experiences in front of those people responsible for their funding was quite humbling and allowed us to give thanks at a far more personal level than ever before.

(3) I was invited to return to RYLA next Spring as a Student Leader as a result of my enthusiasm for the program.

Peer Tutoring

Freshman Student Ambassador

During my junior and senior years, I worked to make the incoming freshman experience a bit less hectic during their Summer Orientation, as well as through a partnership with the Weymouth High School Student Ambassadors Program. I oversee a group of approximately thirty incoming freshman to make a smooth transition between middle and high school life. My support responsibilities range from basic directions to the gym up to profound wisdom on academic rigor and personal safety in upcoming years. Looking back at my freshman experience, such a program did not exist. I am fortunate, in many aspects of my life, to join existing or create newfound opportunities to give support to those coming after me and my peers. Weymouth is a better community for this constant and enduring relationship across age groups.
